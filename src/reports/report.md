# 1. Drivers of Financial Inclusion in Ethiopia

Financial inclusion in Ethiopia is currently being driven more by **policy push** than by organic consumer demand.

### **Mandatory Digital Use Cases**
The digitization of essential services has forced millions into the digital ecosystem. Key examples include:

- Mandatory fuel payment digitization  
- Utility bill digital payment systems  
- Processing of approximately **176 billion ETB** through mandated channels  

These interventions have created artificial spikes in usage rather than voluntary adoption.

---

### **National ID (Fayda) as a KYC Accelerator**
The Fayda National ID system is emerging as a critical enabler:

- Account opening time reduced from **43 minutes â†’ 9 minutes**
- Documentation barriers lowered significantly

This positions Fayda as the most important bridge for rural onboarding.

---

### **Infrastructure Liberalization**
Market competition and infrastructure upgrades are accelerating inclusion capacity:

- Entry of **Safaricom / M-Pesa**
- Rapid 4G expansion by Ethio Telecom
- Increased digital wallet competition

# 2. The Stagnation Mystery  
## (+3pp Account Ownership vs. 65M+ Mobile Money Accounts)

A central puzzle emerges:

> Why did account ownership rise only **+3 percentage points**, despite massive account registrations?

### **Duplicate Wallet Inflation**
The reported **140 million accounts** do not represent unique individuals. Many Ethiopians hold:

- Multiple SIMs  
- Multiple wallets (Telebirr + M-Pesa + bank apps)

Thus, registration growth is not equal to inclusion growth.

---

### **Extremely Low Active Usage (15%)**
Only **1 in 7 registered accounts** is actually active.

Most accounts are opened for:

- One-off mandated payments  
- Short-term compliance transactions  
- Temporary subsidies or fees  

Then abandoned.

---

### **The Device Ceiling**
Infrastructure alone is insufficient when hardware access is limited:

- Smartphone penetration: **21.7%**
- Basic phone ownership: **58%**

Without device access, digital inclusion cannot scale beyond USSD-level transactions.

# 4. Critical Data Gaps Limiting the Analysis

Several missing datasets significantly constrain forecasting accuracy:

---

### **Unique User ID Data**
There is no Fayda-linked dataset showing:

- True unique individuals included  
- Duplicate account filtering  

Without this, inclusion metrics are inflated.

---

### **Rural Transaction Granularity**
Most telecom data is national-level.

We lack:

- Regional transaction density  
- Rural adoption patterns  
- Agricultural last-mile inclusion evidence  

---

### **MSME & Merchant Adoption Data**
A major gap exists in tracking:

- Small merchant digital transition  
- Women-led MSME financial behavior  
- Shift from P2P wallets to merchant/business accounts
